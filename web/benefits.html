Rewarding good behaviour, ensuring equitable participation

www.southwarkcircle.org.uk
Member of the circle who have a skill agree to contribute it in exchange for tokens. The skill could be putting a shelf up, mowing a lawn, giving computer training etc.
Members can all earn and spend tokens, but they are also get some free when they sign up.
Gives everyone a sense of the value of their contribution, many people discover something unexpected to contribute.
Brixton pound
Designed to keep money generated in Brixton local.
Loyalty schemes
Giving rewards that could only be redeemed in a limited number of places
Carbon trading
When you purchase a product or service that emits carbon, as well as spending real cash you also spend your carbon credits (given to you when you start the scheme).
When you run out, you have to pay to get more credits.
Money raised is spent on projects to reduce carbon emissions.
Baby sitting schemes
Circle of people agree to baby sit for one another. Problem is ensuring that everyone contributes equally.
The solution is a system of credits.
